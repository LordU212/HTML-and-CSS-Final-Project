Consider Streaming Players

Injuries and thin depth charts can open temporary and possibly valuable roles in rotations around the league. Last season, a barren backcourt in New York saw Trey Burke earn heavy minutes after the All-Star break en route to 15.9 PPG and 5.9 APG on solid efficiency rates. Some surges are merely fleeting, not every waiver addition we make will become an enduring contributor, yet we can develop a productive mesh of numbers by streaming players throughout the season.

Targeting streaming additions who are facing generous defensive teams like the Kings or Suns proved profitable last season, and a subset of soft defensive foes will develop again this season.
